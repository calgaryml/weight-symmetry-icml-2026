## Yani Ioannou

| <img src="/assets/images/organizers/yani.jpg" alt="Yani Ioannou" height="250px" /> |

| **Affiliation** | [University of Calgary](https://yani.ai) |
| **Biography** | Yani Ioannou is a Schulich Research Chair and Assistant Professor in the Department of Electrical and Software Engineering at the University of Calgary in Alberta, Canada. Yani completed his PhD at the University of Cambridge in 2018 supported by a Microsoft Research PhD Scholarship. Yani leads the Calgary Machine Learning Lab, with research interests including improving sparse neural network training, where the lab's recent work has highlighted the role of weight symmetries. He has served as co-lead organizer of the Workshop on Sparsity in LLMs (SLLM) at ICLR 2025, Sponsorship Chair of the Canadian Conference on AI (CanAI) 2025, and Publicity Co-Chair of the Conference on Parsimony and Learning (CPAL) 2024, amongst other leadership and organizational roles. ## Boris Knyazev

| <img src="/assets/images/organizers/boris.jpg" alt="Boris Knyazev" height="250px" /> |

| **Affiliation** | [Samsung AI Lab Montreal / Université de Montréal](https://bknyaz.github.io/) |
| **Biography** | Boris Knyazev is a Research Scientist at Samsung AI Lab, Montreal, Canada. He is also an Adjunct Professor at the University of Montreal. He completed his PhD at the Machine Learning Research Group, University of Guelph and Vector Institute in 2022. His research interests lie at the intersection of graph neural networks (GNNs), meta-learning and weight space learning with applications in faster optimization algorithms and model compression. He was an invited speaker at the ICLR 2025 Workshop on Weight Space Learning (Neural Network Weights as a New Data Modality). ## Ekaterina Lobacheva

| <img src="/assets/images/organizers/ekaterina.jpg" alt="Ekaterina Lobacheva" height="250px" /> |

| **Affiliation** | [Mila – Quebec AI Institute / Université de Montréal](https://tipt0p.github.io/) |
| **Biography** | Ekaterina Lobacheva is a Postdoctoral Researcher at Mila – Quebec AI Institute and Université de Montréal, working with Prof. Sarath Chandar. She completed her PhD at HSE University, and her postdoctoral research is supported by an IVADO Fellowship. Her research focuses on understanding neural network loss landscapes and training dynamics, the implicit biases of optimization, generalization, as well as ensembling and model merging techniques. Ekaterina has been a key organizer of three editions of the Summer School on Deep Learning and Bayesian Methods (Deep\|Bayes), led research seminars for students at HSE University for many years, and received the NeurIPS Top Reviewer award four times. [[Website](https://tipt0p.github.io/), [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.ru/citations?user=8D4Be1sAAAAJ&hl=en)] |

## Antonio Orvieto

| <img src="/assets/images/organizers/antonio.jpg" alt="Antonio Orvieto" height="250px" /> |

| **Affiliation** | [ELLIS Institute Tübingen / MPI for Intelligent Systems](http://orvi.altervista.org/) |
| **Biography** | Antonio Orvieto studied Control Engineering in Italy and Switzerland. He holds a PhD in Computer Science from ETH Zürich. He is currently a Principal Investigator (PI) at the ELLIS Institute Tübingen and Independent Group Leader of the MPI for Intelligent Systems, where he leads the Deep Models and Optimization group. He received the ETH Medal for Outstanding Doctoral Theses and the Schmidt Sciences AI2050 Early Career Fellowship. His research includes understanding the intricacies of large-scale optimization dynamics and the landscape of modern neural networks. He co-organized the Next Generation of Sequence Modeling Architectures Workshop at ICML 2024 and the Workshop on Principles of Generative Modeling at EurIPS 2025. ## Alexander Theus

| <img src="/assets/images/organizers/alexander.jpg" alt="Alexander Theus" height="250px" /> |

| **Affiliation** | [ETH Zurich / MPI for Intelligent Systems](https://www.linkedin.com/in/alexander-theus/) |
| **Biography** | Alexander Theus is a PhD student in Machine Learning at ETH Zurich and the Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems, advised by Dr. Valentina Boeva and Dr. Antonio Orvieto. His research focuses on the geometry of neural network loss landscapes and weight-space symmetries, with implications for generalization, optimization, and model merging. In his previous work, he showed that accounting for weight-space symmetries beyond permutations enables linear mode connectivity in Transformer models. His research is supported by the Max Planck ETH Center for Learning Systems. He holds an MSc from ETH Zurich and a BSc from the University of Zurich. [[Website](https://www.linkedin.com/in/alexander-theus/), [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=70l0vyEAAAAJ&hl=de)] |
